{# About #}

About the collection

This portal brings together legal records through which Virginians made claims on government, law, and one another during and after the American Revolution. Legislative petitions, pension declarations, and related court records preserve requests, protests, disputes, and sworn accounts from across the Commonwealth.

The records illuminate private claims on the public treasury, the costs and memories of war, contests over wills and estates, demands for religious and personal liberty, and the lives of enslaved Virginians who appear throughout the collection. Each available document links to its Library of Virginia online catalog record, where digitized materials may be viewed.
