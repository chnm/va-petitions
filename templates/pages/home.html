About the collection

This portal gathers two bodies of records through which early Virginians spoke to their government: legislative petitions addressed to the General Assembly, and declarations made by veterans and their families in support of Revolutionary War pensions. Claims, protests, and sworn accounts are arranged here by the county they came from.

The legislative petitions are catalogued first. They range across private claims on the public treasury, contests over wills and estates, and organized protests including a substantial group touching the lives of enslaved Virginians, who appear throughout the record. Each document links to its digitized manuscript so it may be read in full, and the pension declarations are being added county by county.

Records held by the Library of Virginia. Page images served from the Virginia Memory / Rosetta digital repository. Geographic data derived from the catalogue’s county assignments.
